It’s not Digital vs. Paper

Regardless of methods, all couponers have the same goal, to maximize their savings

More on paper methods

  • “Old-schoolers” stick mainly to the Sunday FSI, but are beginning to dabble a little in digital L2C
  • Electronic checkout (Catalinas!) are seen has valuable in both delivery method and savings
    • “They’re put right in my hand. I don’t have to go get them, they’re coming to me.” - Bala Cynwyd, June 2014
  • For many, paper coupons act as a physical reminder of available savings
    • “I need the physical sheet so I remember.” - Bala Cynwyd, April 2015
    • “It helps make me accountable. Digital ones I download and then forget.”
      - Bala Cynwyd, April 2015
  • It’s also a habit long formed and passed down
    • "It’s what you grew up on, it’s what we are used to.  You know, you were used to seeing your parents cutting out coupons. Learned that, stick with it.” - Bala Cynwyd, April 2015

More on digital methods

  • “I use digital coupons because paper ones are small and easy to lose.” – Bala Cynwyd, April 2015
  • Digital methods often liked because shoppers feel more in control and can review on “their terms”
  • Millennials tend to use more digital coupon methods and are more willing to try new technology
  • Email is commonly used to find out about deals or coupons, and drives shoppers to a retailer or 3rd party website
  • Mobile availability is appealing for most “I always have my phone, I never forget it, and when I do, I go back and get it“ – St. Louis, September 2014
    • BUT there is the fear that mobile can be “too much” “When you're ready to sit down and decide what to buy, then it's okay…but if I’m not in the mindset, I do not want to see the alert.” – Boston, September 2014
